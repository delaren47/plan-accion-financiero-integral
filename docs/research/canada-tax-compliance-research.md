## 1. Business Number (BN) Registration

### What is a Business Number?
- **9-digit identifier** issued by Canada Revenue Agency (CRA)
- Required for GST/HST, payroll, corporate tax, and import/export accounts
- Free to obtain
- Single BN for all CRA program accounts

### Program Account Identifiers
Format: `123456789 XX 0001`
- **RC** (Payroll deductions) - for CPP, EI, income tax withholding
- **RT** (GST/HST) - for sales tax remittances
- **RP** (Corporation income tax) - for T2 filing
- **RM** (Import/export)

### How to Obtain
1. **Online (fastest):** CRA Business Registration Online (BRO)
   - https://www.canada.ca/en/revenue-agency/services/tax/businesses/topics/registering-your-business/register.html
   - Immediate BN issuance
   - Can register multiple program accounts simultaneously

2. **By mail:** Form RC1 (Request for a Business Number)

3. **When incorporating:** Can request BN through Corporations Canada or provincial registry

### Official Links
- **CRA Business Number:** https://www.canada.ca/en/services/taxes/business-number.html
- **My Business Account (MBA):** https://www.canada.ca/en/revenue-agency/services/e-services/e-services-businesses/business-account.html

## 2. Corporate Income Tax (T2 Return)

### Filing Deadlines
- **6 months after fiscal year-end**
- Example: December 31 year-end → T2 due June 30
- Electronic filing mandatory for most corporations

### Payment Deadlines
- **2 months after fiscal year-end** (for CCPCs)
- **3 months after fiscal year-end** (for non-CCPCs)
- Example (CCPC): December 31 year-end → tax payment due February 28/29

### Federal Tax Rates (2026)
- **General corporate rate:** 15% (federal only)
- **Small Business Deduction (SBD):** 9% federal rate on first $500,000 of active business income
- **Combined federal small business rate:** 9%
- **Combined federal general rate:** 15%

**CCPC Eligibility for Small Business Deduction:**
- Must be Canadian-controlled
- Must be a private corporation
- Active business income (not investment income)
- $500,000 annual limit (shared among associated corporations)

### Provincial Tax Rates (2026 estimates)
Combined federal + provincial rates for small business income:

| Province | Small Business Rate | General Corporate Rate | Small Business Limit |
|----------|---------------------|------------------------|----------------------|
| **Ontario** | 12.2% (9% + 3.2%) | 26.5% (15% + 11.5%) | First $500,000 |
| **British Columbia** | 11% (9% + 2%) | 27% (15% + 12%) | First $500,000 |
| **Alberta** | 11% (9% + 2%) | 23% (15% + 8%) | First $500,000 |
| **Quebec** | 14.5% (9% + 5.5%) | 26.5% (15% + 11.5%) | First $500,000 |
| **Saskatchewan** | 11% (9% + 2%) | 27% (15% + 12%) | First $600,000 |
| **Manitoba** | 9% (9% + 0%) | 27% (15% + 12%) | First $500,000 |

### How to File
1. **Certified Tax Software:**
   - TurboTax Business
   - UFile Corporate
   - TaxCycle (professional)
   - Profile T2 (professional)

2. **Through CPA:**
   - Recommended for first year and complex situations
   - Cost: $800 - $2,500 for basic CCPC (see Section 9)

3. **EFILE (Electronic filing):**
   - Mandatory for corporations with gross revenue over $1M
   - Recommended for all to speed up processing

### Required Schedules (minimum)
- Schedule 1: Net Income (Loss) for Income Tax Purposes
- Schedule 100: Balance Sheet Information
- Schedule 125: Income Statement Information
- Schedule 200: Taxable Income
- GIFI (General Index of Financial Information)

## 3. GST/HST (Sales Tax)

### Registration Threshold
- **Mandatory:** When gross revenue exceeds $30,000 in any single quarter or cumulatively over 4 consecutive quarters
- **Voluntary:** Can register below threshold to claim Input Tax Credits (ITCs)
- **Recommendation for consultants:** Register immediately to claim ITCs on business expenses

### HST vs GST by Province (2026 rates)

**HST Provinces (Harmonized Sales Tax):**
| Province | HST Rate | Federal Portion | Provincial Portion |
|----------|----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Ontario | 13% | 5% | 8% |
| New Brunswick | 15% | 5% | 10% |
| Newfoundland & Labrador | 15% | 5% | 10% |
| Nova Scotia | 15% | 5% | 10% |
| Prince Edward Island | 15% | 5% | 10% |

**GST + PST Provinces:**
| Province | GST | PST | Combined |
|----------|-----|-----|----------|
| British Columbia | 5% | 7% | 12% |
| Saskatchewan | 5% | 6% | 11% |
| Manitoba | 5% | 7% | 12% |
| Quebec | 5% GST | 9.975% QST | ~15% |

**GST Only:**
| Province/Territory | Rate |
|-------------------|------|
| Alberta | 5% GST only |
| Yukon | 5% GST only |
| Northwest Territories | 5% GST only |
| Nunavut | 5% GST only |

### Filing Frequency
Determined by annual revenue:

- **Annual filing:** Revenue ≤ $1,500,000
  - Due date: 3 months after fiscal year-end

- **Quarterly filing:** Revenue $1,500,001 - $6,000,000
  - Due date: 1 month after quarter-end

- **Monthly filing:** Revenue > $6,000,000
  - Due date: 1 month after month-end

**Note:** Can elect quarterly/monthly even if annual eligible

### Input Tax Credits (ITCs)
- **Can claim:** GST/HST paid on business expenses (software, equipment, contractors, etc.)
- **Cannot claim:** Personal expenses, exempt supplies
- **Requirement:** Must have valid receipts/invoices showing GST/HST paid
- **Timing:** Claim in the same period expense was incurred

### Deadlines and Penalties

**Filing Deadlines:**
- Annual filers: 3 months after fiscal year-end
- Quarterly filers: 1 month after quarter-end
- Monthly filers: 1 month after month-end

**Payment Deadlines:** Same as filing deadlines

**Penalties:**
- **Late filing:** 1% of balance owing + 0.25% per month (max 12 months)
- **Late payment:** 1% on first day + 0.25% per month
- **Gross negligence:** 50% of tax evaded (for intentional errors)
- **Interest:** CRA prescribed rate (typically 5-10% annually) compounded daily

## 4. Corporate Tax Instalments

### When Required
- If total federal and provincial tax payable exceeds **$3,000** in current or prior year
- Applies to most profitable CCPCs after first year

### Payment Schedule
- **Monthly instalments** due on the **last day of each month**
- 12 instalments per year
- Instalments offset final tax payable when filing T2

### Calculation Methods

**Option 1: Prior-year method**
- 1/12 of prior year's total tax payable each month

**Option 2: Current-year estimate**
- 1/12 of estimated current year tax payable
- Risky if you underestimate (instalment interest applies)

**Option 3: Prior-year/current-year combination**
- First 2 months: 1/12 of second prior year's tax
- Remaining 10 months: (current estimate - first 2 months) / 10

### Instalment Interest
- Charged if instalments insufficient
- Rate: CRA prescribed rate + 4% (typically 9-14%)
- Can offset against interest owed to you if overpaid

## 5. Provincial Obligations

### Extra-Provincial Registration

**When Required:**
If operating in a province other than province of incorporation, must register as "extra-provincial" corporation.

**Example:** Federally incorporated, but conducting business in Ontario → must register in Ontario

**Key Provinces:**

**Ontario:**
- Extra-provincial registration: ~$330 filing fee
- Annual return required
- ServiceOntario portal: https://www.ontario.ca/page/register-or-make-changes-your-business
- Corporations Information Act compliance

**British Columbia:**
- Extra-provincial registration: $350 online, $400 paper
- Annual report required ($40.71 fee)
- BC Registry Services: https://www.bcregistry.gov.bc.ca/

**Alberta:**
- Extra-provincial registration: $100 online, $127 paper
- Annual return required ($20)
- Alberta Corporate Registry: https://www.alberta.ca/corporate-registry

**Quebec:**
- Extra-provincial registration: ~$378
- Annual updating declaration required
- Registre des entreprises du Québec: https://www.quebec.ca/en/businesses-and-self-employed-individuals/

### Provincial Annual Returns

Most provinces require annual filings even without extra-provincial status:

| Province | Annual Return Fee | Due Date |
|----------|------------------|----------|
| Ontario | $0 (included in initial fee) | 60 days before incorporation anniversary |
| BC | $40.71 | Within 2 months after anniversary |
| Alberta | $20 | On or before anniversary date |
| Quebec | ~$42 | Annual update required |

### Costs Summary (Extra-Provincial Operations)

**Scenario: Federal incorporation + operate in ON, BC, AB**
- Federal incorporation: $200
- Ontario registration: $330
- BC registration: $350
- Alberta registration: $100
- **Total initial:** $980
- **Annual ongoing:** ~$100-150 (annual returns)

## 6. Payroll Requirements (If Hiring Employees)

### CPP (Canada Pension Plan) Rates 2026

**Employee contribution:** 5.95% on pensionable earnings
**Employer contribution:** 5.95% (matches employee)
**Combined:** 11.9%

**Contribution limits (2026 estimates):**
- Basic exemption: $3,500
- Maximum pensionable earnings: $68,500
- Maximum contribution (employee): $3,867.50
- Maximum contribution (employer): $3,867.50

### EI (Employment Insurance) Rates 2026

**Employee premium:** 1.66% of insurable earnings
**Employer premium:** 2.324% (1.4 × employee rate)

**Maximum insurable earnings (2026 estimate):** $63,200
**Maximum employee premium:** $1,049.12
**Maximum employer premium:** $1,468.77

### T4/T4A/T5 Slip Requirements

**T4 Slips (Employment Income):**
- **Who:** All employees
- **Filing deadline:** Last day of February following tax year
- **Distribution to employee:** Last day of February
- **Format:** Paper or electronic (with consent)

**T4A Slips (Self-Employed/Contract Income):**
- **Who:** Contractors paid >$500 for services
- **Filing deadline:** Last day of February
- **Note:** Most consultants issue T4A to subcontractors, not employees

**T5 Slips (Investment Income):**
- **Who:** Shareholders receiving dividends
- **Filing deadline:** Last day of February
- **Includes:** Eligible dividends, other than eligible dividends, capital dividends

### Remittance Schedules

**Based on average monthly withholding amount (AMWA):**

- **Quarterly remitter:** AMWA $1,000 - $3,000
  - Due: 15th day after quarter-end

- **Monthly remitter:** AMWA $3,000 - $25,000
  - Due: 15th day of following month

- **Accelerated remitter (threshold 1):** AMWA $25,000 - $100,000
  - Due: Pay periods 1-15 of month → due 25th
  - Pay periods 16-end → due 10th of next month

- **Accelerated remitter (threshold 2):** AMWA >$100,000
  - Due: Within 4 days of pay period end

**Penalties for late remittance:**
- 3% if 1-3 days late
- 5% if 4-5 days late
- 7% if 6-7 days late
- 10% if more than 7 days late
- 20% if repeated failures

## 7. Foreign Reporting

### T1135 (Foreign Income Verification Statement)

**When Required:**
- Total cost of "specified foreign property" exceeds **$100,000 CAD** at any time during the year
- Includes: foreign bank accounts, foreign stocks, foreign real estate (not personal use), loans to non-residents

**Who Files:**
- Individuals, corporations, trusts resident in Canada

**Filing Deadline:**
- Corporations: Same as T2 return deadline
- Individuals: Same as T1 return deadline (April 30)

**Penalties:**
- $25 per day late (minimum $100, maximum $2,500)
- Gross negligence: $500/month (max $12,000)

**Official Form:** Form T1135
**Link:** https://www.canada.ca/en/revenue-agency/services/forms-publications/forms/t1135.html

### T1134 (Information Return Relating to Controlled and Not-Controlled Foreign Affiliates)

**When Required:**
- Corporation has shares in foreign affiliates
- Foreign affiliate: Non-resident corporation where Canadian corp owns ≥1% shares

**Filing Deadline:**
- 15 months after tax year-end

**Penalties:**
- $25 per day late (minimum $100, maximum $2,500 per return)

**Note for Consulting Business:**
- Unlikely to apply unless operating foreign subsidiaries
- T1135 more relevant if holding US/international investments >$100k

## 8. Business Registration/Incorporation

### Federal vs Provincial Incorporation

**Federal Incorporation (Corporations Canada):**

**Pros:**
- Name protection across all of Canada
- Can operate in any province (still need extra-provincial registration)
- Professional image for national/international clients
- Easier to expand interprovincially

**Cons:**
- Higher initial cost ($200 online, $250 paper)
- Must still register extra-provincially in each operating province
- More complex annual compliance (federal + provincial)

**Process:**
1. NUANS name search report (~$20-50)
2. Online filing through Corporations Canada portal
3. Articles of Incorporation
4. Initial registered office address required
5. Processing: 1-5 business days online

**Provincial Incorporation (e.g., Ontario, BC, Alberta):**

**Pros:**
- Lower initial cost (varies by province)
- Simpler if only operating in one province
- Single jurisdiction compliance (if staying in-province)

**Cons:**
- Name protection only in province of incorporation
- Must re-incorporate or register extra-provincially to expand
- Less flexible for growth

**Provincial Costs:**
- **Ontario:** $300 online, $360 paper (Ontario Business Registry)
- **British Columbia:** $350 online, $400 paper
- **Alberta:** $275 online, $340 paper

### Corporations Canada Portal
- **Main portal:** https://ised-isde.canada.ca/site/corporations-canada/en
- **Online filing:** https://www.ic.gc.ca/app/scr/cc/CorporationsCanada/hm.html

### Requirements for Incorporation

**Documents needed:**
- Articles of Incorporation
- NUANS name search (if not numbered company)
- Registered office address (Canadian address required)
- Board of directors (minimum 25% must be Canadian residents for federal)
- Share structure

**Post-Incorporation:**
- Organizational meeting (director resolutions)
- Share certificates
- Corporate minute book
- Register with CRA for BN and program accounts

## 10. Penalties (Summary)

### Late T2 Filing
- **5% of unpaid tax** (due date)
- **+1% per month** late (max 12 months)
- **Double penalties** if repeated failures within 3 years

**Example:** $10,000 tax owing, 3 months late
- Initial penalty: $500 (5%)
- Monthly penalty: $300 (1% × 3 months)
- **Total penalty:** $800
- **Plus interest** at prescribed rate

### Late GST/HST Filing
- **1% of balance owing** (first day late)
- **+0.25% per month** late (max 12 months)
- **Gross negligence:** 50% of tax evaded

**Example:** $5,000 GST owing, 2 months late
- Initial penalty: $50 (1%)
- Monthly penalty: $25 (0.25% × 2 months)
- **Total penalty:** $75
- **Plus interest**

### Late Payroll Remittances
- **3%** if 1-3 days late
- **5%** if 4-5 days late
- **7%** if 6-7 days late
- **10%** if more than 7 days late
- **20%** for repeated failures

**Example:** $2,000 payroll remittance, 10 days late
- **Penalty:** $200 (10% of $2,000)
- **Plus interest**

### Late T4/T5 Slips
- **$25 per day** late (per return, not per slip)
- **Minimum:** $100
- **Maximum:** $2,500

**Example:** T4 slips 30 days late
- **Penalty:** $750 ($25 × 30 days)

### Failure to File T1135 (Foreign Property)
- **$25 per day** late
- **Minimum:** $100
- **Maximum:** $2,500
- **Gross negligence:** $500/month (max $12,000)

### Interest on Overdue Amounts
- **Prescribed rate** (set quarterly by CRA)
- **Current rate (Q4 2025):** ~7-8% annually
- **Compounded daily**
- **Non-deductible** for corporate tax purposes

## Tax Optimization Strategies for CCPC Consulting Business

### Salary vs Dividend Strategy

**Salary:**
- **Pros:** Creates RRSP room, CPP contributions (future pension), deductible expense for corporation
- **Cons:** Subject to CPP/EI (if not controlling shareholder), personal income tax

**Dividends:**
- **Pros:** No CPP/EI, dividend tax credit reduces personal tax, more flexible timing
- **Cons:** No RRSP room, no CPP credits, must have retained earnings

**Optimal Strategy (2026):**
- Pay salary up to small business limit ($500,000) to maximize corporate deduction
- Top up with eligible dividends for tax efficiency
- Consult CPA for personal situation (depends on province, other income)

### Income Splitting (If Applicable)

**Spousal involvement:**
- If spouse performs legitimate services, can pay reasonable salary
- Dividend income splitting limited by TOSI rules (Tax on Split Income)
- Must meet "excluded shares" criteria or work 20+ hours/week in business

**TOSI exceptions:**
- Age 25+, contributed property/labor
- Age 18-24, reasonable return on invested capital
- Dividends from "excluded business" (active involvement)

### Small Business Deduction Optimization

**To maximize 9% federal rate:**
- Keep active business income under $500,000
- Avoid associated corporations (splits $500k limit)
- Minimize investment income (claws back SBD over $50k)

### Tax Deferral

**Corporate tax vs personal tax:**
- CCPC rate: 12.2% (ON), 11% (BC/AB) on first $500k
- Personal rate: 20-50% depending on province, income level
- **Deferral opportunity:** Leave profits in corporation, pay later when needed or at lower personal income

## Glossary

**AMWA:** Average Monthly Withholding Amount - determines payroll remittance frequency

**BN:** Business Number - 9-digit CRA identifier

**CCPC:** Canadian-Controlled Private Corporation - private corp controlled by Canadian residents, eligible for small business deduction

**CPP:** Canada Pension Plan - mandatory retirement savings for employees

**EI:** Employment Insurance - mandatory insurance for employees

**GIFI:** General Index of Financial Information - standardized financial statement format for T2

**GST:** Goods and Services Tax - 5% federal sales tax

**HST:** Harmonized Sales Tax - combined federal+provincial sales tax in participating provinces

**ITC:** Input Tax Credit - GST/HST recovery on business purchases

**NUANS:** Newly Upgraded Automated Name Search - required name search for incorporation

**PST:** Provincial Sales Tax - provincial-only sales tax (BC, SK, MB, QC)

**SBD:** Small Business Deduction - reduced 9% federal tax rate on first $500k active business income for CCPCs

**TOSI:** Tax on Split Income - rules limiting income splitting with family members
